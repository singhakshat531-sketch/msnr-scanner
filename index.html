MSNR + CRT STRATEGY GUIDE
Malaysian Support & Resistance (MSNR)
MSNR uses candle close prices to identify key levels:

V-Level (Support) — A close price lower than N candles on both sides. The level is fresh if no subsequent close has gone below it. Price is expected to bounce or reverse here.

A-Level (Resistance) — A close price higher than N candles on both sides. Fresh if no close has gone above it. Price is expected to reject here.

HSL (Historical Significance) — Level was tested 2+ times in the past, making it high-confluence.

Timeframe hierarchy: 1D sets overall bias → 4H identifies key trade zones → 1H provides entry.
Candle Range Theory (CRT) Entry
CRT is the 1H entry model applied at the 4H MSNR level. It follows the AMD cycle:

A — Accumulation: 2–10 consecutive 1H candles form a tight range (≤1.5% wide) at or touching the 4H level. This is smart money building a position quietly.

M — Manipulation (Sweep): A 1H candle's wick pierces BEYOND the 4H level (below support for longs, above resistance for shorts) to trigger retail stop-losses. The candle CLOSES BACK inside the range. This is the liquidity grab — institutions filling orders.

D — Distribution (Break): The next 1H candle closes beyond the OPPOSITE side of the accumulation range with a strong body (≥30% body ratio). The market now distributes in the real direction.

Entry: Place limit order at the 4H MSNR level after Phase C confirms.
SL: Below/above the sweep wick extreme.
TP1: 3R from entry. Close 50%, move SL to BE.
TP2: Nearest 1D MSNR level in trade direction.
